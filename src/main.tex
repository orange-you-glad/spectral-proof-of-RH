\begin{center}
\textit{“The spectrum is the zero set.”}
\end{center}
\vspace{1.5em}

\begin{abstract}
We construct a canonical compact, self-adjoint, trace-class operator
\[
L_{\mathrm{sym}} \in \mathcal{C}_1(H_{\Psi_\alpha}),
\]
on the exponentially weighted Hilbert space \( H_{\Psi_\alpha} = L^2(\mathbb{R}, e^{\alpha|x|} \, dx) \), for fixed \( \alpha > \pi \). Its Carleman \(\zeta\)-regularized Fredholm determinant satisfies the canonical identity
\[
\det\nolimits_{\zeta}(I - \lambda L_{\mathrm{sym}}) = \frac{\Xi(\tfrac{1}{2} + i\lambda)}{\Xi(\tfrac{1}{2})},
\]
where \( \Xi(s) \) is the completed Riemann zeta function.

This determinant is entire of order one and exponential type \( \pi \), and encodes the nontrivial zeros \( \rho \) of \( \zeta(s) \) via the spectral map
\[
\mu_\rho := \tfrac{1}{i}(\rho - \tfrac{1}{2}) \in \operatorname{Spec}(L_{\mathrm{sym}}),
\]
preserving multiplicities. The operator \( L_{\mathrm{sym}} \) arises as the trace-norm limit of a mollified convolution family, with kernels derived from the inverse Fourier transform of \( \Xi \).

We prove holomorphy of the heat semigroup \( e^{-t L_{\mathrm{sym}}^2} \), establish short-time trace asymptotics with a logarithmic singularity, and recover the zero-counting function via Korevaar’s Tauberian theory. These analytic results collectively yield the equivalence:
\[
\mathrm{RH} \iff \operatorname{Spec}(L_{\mathrm{sym}}) \subset \mathbb{R}.
\]

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Trace-class threshold:} The exponential weight \( \alpha > \pi \) is both necessary and sufficient for trace-class regularity of the inverse Fourier kernel; see \lemref{lem:trace_class_failure_alpha_leq_pi}.

\medskip

\noindent
\textbf{Extensions:} Appendices~\ref{app:heat_kernel_refinements} and~\ref{app:additional_structures} explore numerical refinement, spectral generalization to automorphic \( L \)-functions, and conjectural motivic lifts.
\end{abstract}

\begin{theorem}[Spectral Equivalence with the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis]
\label{thm:spec_real_equiv_grh}
Let \( \pi \in \mathcal{A}_{\mathrm{cusp}}(\GL_n) \), and assume:
\begin{itemize}
  \item the analytic properties of \( \Xi(s, \pi) \) from \defref{def:analytic_properties_XiPi},
  \item the exponential kernel decay condition from \defref{def:kernel_decay_condition},
  \item the determinant identity from \thmref{thm:spectral_determinant_identity_Lpi}.
\end{itemize}

Then the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis for \( L(s, \pi) \) is equivalent to the spectral inclusion
\[
\Spec(\Lsympi) \subset \R.
\]

That is, all nontrivial zeros \( \rho \in \C \) of \( \Xi(s, \pi) \) satisfy \( \Re(\rho) = \tfrac{1}{2} \) if and only if all eigenvalues of \( \Lsympi \) are real.
\end{theorem}
